## Occupational Safety

**7. What is occupational safety?**  
The set of actions, standards, and practices aimed at protecting the health and physical well-being of workers by identifying hazards and implementing preventive measures.

**8. Why is occupational safety important for a company?**  
Beyond being required by Brazilian law, it protects people, reduces accidents and absenteeism, increases productivity, and avoids fines, lawsuits, and reputational damage.

**9. What are Brazil's Regulatory Standards (NRs)?**  
Rules issued by the Ministry of Labor and Employment that all companies must follow. There are 38 NRs covering topics such as CIPA (NR-5), PPE (NR-6), Environmental Risk Prevention (NR-9), hazardous activities (NR-15), and others.

**10. What does an occupational safety engineer do?**  
Identifies hazards, proposes preventive measures, prepares technical reports (LTCAT, PPR), trains teams, and ensures regulatory compliance.

**11. How is occupational safety enforcement conducted in Brazil?**  
By Labor Inspectors (Auditores Fiscais do Trabalho) of the Ministry of Labor, through scheduled and unannounced inspections with fines for violations.

**12. What is the history of occupational safety in Brazil?**  
Gained momentum with the CLT (1943), evolved with the NRs (1978) and the Federal Constitution (1988). Brazil has one of the most comprehensive occupational health and safety regulatory systems in Latin America.

**13. How can I improve occupational safety at my company?**  
Risk diagnostic, control measures, procedures, worker training, and continuous monitoring of performance indicators.

**14. What is occupational risk management and how does it work?**  
The process of identifying, evaluating, and controlling hazards. Since 2021, the GRO (NR-1) is mandatory in Brazil.

**15. How does risk management affect company productivity?**  
Safer workplaces reduce absenteeism, rework, and stoppages. Every real invested in prevention generates far greater savings in accident-related costs.

**16. What is an accident and near-miss investigation?**  
A technical analysis to understand root causes and define corrective actions to prevent recurrence.

**17. How is an occupational safety audit conducted?**  
Includes site visits, interviews, document review, and a report identifying conformities, non-conformities, and improvement recommendations.

**18. What is a forensic occupational safety assessment (perícia)?**  
A formal technical evaluation by a qualified professional, typically in judicial or administrative proceedings, producing a legal-weight technical report.

**19. What safety procedures can Exato create or review?**  
Contractor EHS Manual, Lockout/Tagout, Work at Heights, Confined Spaces, PPE, Accident Investigation, Permit to Work, Machine Guarding, Chemical Products Management, Radiation Protection Plan, among others.

**20. What is Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)?**  
Any device worn individually to protect against risks: hard hats, gloves, safety glasses, hearing protection, respirators, safety boots, and harnesses. Providing appropriate PPE is a legal employer obligation under Brazilian law.

**21. Which PPE is best suited for each type of hazard?**  
Noise: hearing protection; dust/gases: respirators; head impact: hard hats; electrical: insulating gloves and boots; working at heights: full-body harness. All must carry a CA (Certificate of Approval) from the Ministry of Labor.

**22. Where can ionizing radiation PPE be obtained?**  
From distributors specializing in radiological protection. Exato advises on required equipment and recommends certified suppliers.

**23. What is safety culture and why does it matter?**  
The set of values, attitudes, and behaviors shared by everyone in the organization regarding safety. In companies with strong safety culture, people follow safe practices by conviction, not obligation.

**24. What role does leadership play in building a safety culture?**  
Leadership is the most critical factor. Managers who demonstrate by example that safety is a genuine priority create environments where everyone feels responsible.

**25. Do changes in safety culture produce measurable results?**  
Yes. Significant reductions in accident and absenteeism rates, improved engagement, and lower operating costs, typically visible within 12 to 24 months.

**26. How do training programs help change safety culture?**  
They foster reflection, shift risk perception, and strengthen individual accountability.

**27. Which occupational safety and environmental training programs are mandatory in Brazil?**  
Depending on sector: NR-10 (electrical), NR-11 (material transport), NR-12 (machinery), NR-18 (civil construction), NR-20 (flammable materials), NR-33 (confined spaces), NR-35 (working at heights), among others.

**28. What is DDS (Daily Safety Briefing)?**  
A brief conversation (5 to 15 minutes) at the start of a shift to address a safety topic relevant to that day's activities.

**29. What does EHS stand for?**  
Environment, Health, and Safety. In Brazil, also referred to as SMS or SSMA.

## Environment

**33. Does Exato monitor compliance with environmental license conditions?**  
Yes. Reviews conditions, organizes deadlines, guides required actions, and prepares progress reports for Brazilian environmental agencies.

**34. What is the environment?**  
All conditions and interactions involving living beings and natural resources — water, air, soil, flora, fauna — and human-made elements.

**35. What are the types of environment?**  
Natural, artificial, cultural, and occupational.

**36. Why is it important to protect the environment?**  
Ensures natural resources remain available for future generations. For businesses, it also reduces regulatory risk, enhances reputation, and generates savings.

**37. What is CONAMA?**  
Brazil's National Environment Council — the principal advisory and decision-making body of the National Environment System, responsible for establishing environmental standards.

**38. What is CETESB?**  
São Paulo State Environmental Agency — responsible for environmental licensing, enforcement, and monitoring in the state of São Paulo, Brazil.

**39. What is IBAMA?**  
Brazilian Institute for the Environment and Renewable Natural Resources — Brazil's federal environmental agency for licensing, enforcement, and protected area management.

**40. What is an Environmental Assessment?**  
A technical survey of environmental conditions of a site or industrial operation — evaluating soil, water, air, waste, and legal compliance.

**41. What is environmental licensing in Brazil and when is it required?**  
The process by which Brazilian authorities authorize installation and operation of projects with potential environmental impact. Mandatory for potentially polluting activities.

**42. What is the difference between LP, LI, and LO?**  
LP (Prior License): approves feasibility. LI (Installation License): authorizes construction. LO (Operating License): allows operations to begin.

**43. How can I obtain an environmental license for my company?**  
Identify the competent agency, prepare technical studies and documentation, and submit to the agency. Exato manages the entire process.

**44. What is the validity period of an environmental license in Brazil?**  
LP: up to 5 years. LI: up to 6 years. LO: 4 to 10 years. Operating with an expired license is a regulatory infraction subject to fines and suspension.

**45. What is an EIA/RIMA in Brazil?**  
EIA (Environmental Impact Study): detailed technical document analyzing project impacts. RIMA: accessible plain-language version for the general public.

**46. What is CADRI and when does my company need one?**  
Certificate for the Movement of Environmentally Relevant Waste — issued by CETESB, authorizes transport of hazardous waste within the state of São Paulo.

**47. How can I appeal an environmental fine in Brazil?**  
By submitting an administrative defense to the issuing agency, grounded in technical and legal arguments. Exato prepares technical defenses with solid evidence.

**48. What environmental obligations apply to a company installing a diesel generator in Brazil?**  
Environmental license (depending on capacity), CETESB notification for atmospheric emissions, spill containment plan, noise control, and in some cases an air quality assessment.

**49. What is solid waste management?**  
All actions to collect, transport, treat, and properly dispose of waste, safely and in compliance with Brazilian law.

**50. What is a PGRS and when is it required?**  
Solid Waste Management Plan — required by Brazil's National Solid Waste Policy (PNRS, Law 12.305/2010) for hazardous waste generators, healthcare facilities, and industrial companies.

**51. What must a company do to comply with Brazil's PNRS?**  
Adopt reduction, reuse, and recycling practices; prepare a PGRS; participate in reverse logistics systems; contract licensed waste transporters and disposal companies.

**52. How are Class I hazardous waste materials classified and disposed of in Brazil?**  
Include solvents, used lubricating oils, fluorescent lamps, and contaminated packaging. Disposal by licensed company with issuance of MTR (Waste Transport Manifest).

**53. What is a waste manifest (MTR) and how is it completed in Brazil?**  
Electronic document in the SINIR system (federal) or SIGOR (São Paulo state) tracking waste from generator to final destination. Exato guides correct completion.

**54. What is reverse logistics and what are companies' obligations in Brazil?**  
System for returning products and packaging to manufacturers after use. Mandatory for batteries, tires, lubricating oils, pesticides, electronics, and packaging.

**55. How should companies properly dispose of electronic waste?**  
Send to collectors or drop-off points linked to the electronics reverse logistics system. Exato identifies certified and licensed facilities.

**56. What is liquid effluent management?**  
Control of industrial wastewater to ensure proper treatment before discharge, with monitoring and compliance with Brazilian environmental standards.

**57. What environmental procedures can Exato create or review?**  
PGRS, Emergency Action Plans (PAE), Environmental Monitoring Programs, Effluent and Emissions Control Procedures, License Compliance Reports, and Environmental Education Programs.

**58. What is a water use grant (outorga) and when does my company need one?**  
Authorization by Brazilian authorities for water use — withdrawal from rivers or aquifers, or effluent discharge. Mandatory when volumes exceed legal thresholds.

**59. How should a company conduct water monitoring?**  
Periodic water sample collection at intake and discharge points, laboratory analysis, and comparison against regulatory standards.

**60. How can production facilities reduce water consumption?**  
Mapping high-consumption points, reusing process water, replacing water-intensive technologies, pipeline maintenance, and water conservation programs.
